Our Story

Since being established in 2014, The Detroit Psychedelic Society has been known to push the envelope so to speak in moving forward all positive energy as it relates to Psychedelics. It's this standard of excellence that has provided the impetus for us to grow into the family and community based business we are today.

We believe that information and education always comes first - and that means being responsible as leaders in this growing field of study by offering exceptional products and exceptional services.

Mission & Core Values

Our Mission

To advance understanding, education, and responsible exploration of psychedelics while fostering community connection and supporting individual growth and healing.

"Transforming consciousness, one mind at a time."

Core Values

  • Education - Information and education always comes first
  • Safety - Promoting responsible exploration and harm reduction
  • Community - Building connection across Detroit's diverse population
  • Research - Supporting scientific advancement and evidence-based approaches
  • Inclusivity - Creating welcoming spaces for all backgrounds and experiences

Vision

A Detroit community where psychedelic education, responsible use, and integration support are accessible to all who seek healing and growth.

Visual Identity

Logo

Our logo combines elements of Detroit's geography with sacred geometry and cosmic elements. The profile silhouette of Michigan with a focus on Detroit creates immediate geographic identity, while the circular design and emanating lines represent expansion of consciousness and community connection.

Logo Usage Guidelines:

  • Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "D" in Detroit
  • Never stretch or distort the logo proportions
  • Primary logo should appear in white on dark backgrounds or deep blue on light backgrounds
  • Minimum size for digital: 100px width
  • Minimum size for print: 1 inch width

Target Audience

The Curious Seeker

Individuals interested in learning about psychedelics from an educational standpoint, often new to the subject and seeking reliable information.

  • Age: 25-45
  • Values education and evidence-based approaches
  • Often has interest in wellness, mindfulness, or personal growth

The Healing Journeyer

People seeking alternative approaches to mental health, trauma healing, or personal transformation through psychedelic experiences.

  • Age: 30-55
  • May have tried conventional therapies with limited success
  • Values community support and integration practices

The Experienced Explorer

Those with existing psychedelic experience seeking community, integration support, or deeper understanding of their practices.

  • Age: 25-65
  • Has multiple psychedelic experiences
  • Seeking deeper knowledge and community connections

Detroit-Specific Demographics

Our target audience reflects Detroit's diverse communities across races, socioeconomic backgrounds, and neighborhoods. We aim to be accessible to the entire Detroit metropolitan area, with special focus on communities historically excluded from psychedelic discourse.

Community Needs Assessment

Education

78% of our community members express strong interest in science-based psychedelic education that bridges traditional and modern approaches.

Safe Gathering Spaces

65% seek physical locations for community connection and in-person events, emphasizing the importance of set and setting.

Integration Support

92% identify integration practices as critical for long-term beneficial outcomes from psychedelic experiences.

Legal Awareness

83% want clear information on the current legal status of psychedelics in Detroit and Michigan, with updates on decriminalization efforts.

Detroit & Michigan Context

Local Significance

Detroit's Psychedelic Renaissance

Detroit has emerged as a leader in the modern psychedelic movement, with community organizers working to create accessible education and healing spaces.

Urban Renewal & Personal Transformation

Our brand draws parallels between Detroit's revitalization and the personal transformation possible through psychedelic experiences, highlighting themes of renewal and rebirth.

Multicultural Heritage

Our visual identity honors Detroit's diverse cultural heritage, drawing inspiration from the city's rich history of music, art, and community organizing.

Legal Context

Detroit has decriminalized entheogenic plants (2021), alongside Ann Arbor, Hazel Park, Ferndale, and Ypsilanti. Our communications always include current, accurate information on legal status and responsible approaches.
