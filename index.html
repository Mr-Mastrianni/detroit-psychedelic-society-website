Plant Medicine Resources

Learn about different entheogenic plants and fungi, their properties, historical uses, and safety considerations. Knowledge empowers responsible exploration.

Psilocybin Mushrooms

Containing the compound psilocybin, these fungi have been used for centuries in spiritual and healing contexts. Modern research shows promising results for treating depression, anxiety, and addiction.

Effects may include:

  • Visual and sensory enhancement
  • Emotional insights and release
  • Mystical or spiritual experiences
  • Altered perception of time

Safety note: Set, setting, and integration are crucial. Not recommended for those with certain psychiatric conditions or on specific medications.

DMT & Ayahuasca

DMT (Dimethyltryptamine) is found in many plants and is the active compound in Ayahuasca, a traditional brew used in Amazonian shamanic practices for healing and spiritual connection.

Effects may include:

  • Profound visionary experiences
  • Encounter with entities or archetypes
  • Deep emotional processing
  • Spiritual or existential insights

Safety note: Ayahuasca has serious drug interactions with many medications. Always consult with knowledgeable practitioners and disclose all medications.

MDMA

Though synthetic rather than a plant medicine, MDMA is showing remarkable potential in therapy for PTSD. MAPS research has advanced MDMA-assisted therapy toward FDA approval for treatment-resistant PTSD.

Effects may include:

  • Increased empathy and openness
  • Reduced fear response
  • Enhanced emotional processing
  • Feelings of connection and trust

Safety note: Hyperthermia and dehydration are risks. Integration support is essential for therapeutic benefit. Not recommended for those with heart conditions.

Peyote & San Pedro

These mescaline-containing cacti have been used in Native American and South American healing traditions for thousands of years. They are sacred plants with deep cultural significance.

Effects may include:

  • Vivid visual experiences
  • Deep introspective insights
  • Connection to nature
  • Physical purging and cleansing

Safety note: Peyote is endangered and sacred to Native American traditions. Cultural respect and ecological awareness are essential considerations.

Integration & Growth

Integration transforms temporary experiences into lasting positive change. Discover practices and resources to help you make meaning from your journeys and apply insights to everyday life.

  • Journaling

    Writing about your experiences helps process insights and emotions while creating a record you can revisit. Try stream-of-consciousness writing, guided prompts, or visual journaling to capture different aspects of your experience.

  • Meditation & Mindfulness

    Regular meditation helps maintain connection to the insights and states of consciousness accessed during psychedelic experiences. Practice mindful awareness of thoughts, emotions, and sensations to deepen self-understanding.

  • Creative Expression

    Art, music, dance, and other creative practices can help express and process experiences that may be difficult to put into words. Allow creative flow without judgment to access deeper insights and emotional processing.

  • Nature Connection

    Spending time in natural settings can help maintain the sense of connection and wonder often experienced during psychedelic journeys. Practice mindful walks, forest bathing, or simply sitting quietly in nature.

  • Integration Specialists

    Professional support from therapists and coaches familiar with psychedelic integration can be invaluable. We maintain a resource list of psychedelic-informed therapists and integration specialists in the Detroit area.

  • Reading Materials

    Books, articles, and research papers can provide frameworks for understanding and integrating experiences. Our curated reading list covers integration practices, psychedelic science, spirituality, and consciousness.

  • Podcasts & Videos

    Audio and visual resources featuring experts in the field can provide valuable insights and practices. Our media library includes interviews, lectures, guided meditations, and integration tutorials.

Detroit & Michigan Resources

Information specific to our local context, including legal status, community initiatives, and regional resources for the conscious psychedelic community.

Legal Landscape

Understanding the legal context is essential for responsible engagement. Here's the current status of psychedelic policy in our region:

2020

Ann Arbor Decriminalization

In September 2020, Ann Arbor City Council unanimously passed an ordinance to decriminalize entheogenic plants, including psilocybin mushrooms, ayahuasca, ibogaine, and mescaline-containing cacti.

2021

Detroit Decriminalization

In November 2021, Detroit voters passed Proposal E, which decriminalized entheogenic plants, making enforcement of laws against these substances the city's lowest law enforcement priority.

2022

Hazel Park Decriminalization

In March 2022, Hazel Park became the third Michigan city to decriminalize entheogenic plants and fungi, following the lead of Ann Arbor and Detroit.

2024

Statewide Initiatives

Ongoing efforts to decriminalize or create regulated access to certain psychedelics at the state level. Michigan lawmakers have introduced legislation related to psychedelic therapy for PTSD treatment.

Important Note: Decriminalization does not mean legalization. While these policies reduce prosecution priority, psychedelic substances remain federally controlled. Our society advocates for responsible engagement within the current legal framework while supporting evidence-based policy reform.
